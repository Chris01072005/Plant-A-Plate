How to Plant Your Seed-Infused Plate

  1. Break your used plate into smaller pieces after your event.
  2. Choose a spot with plenty of sunlight and loose, moist soil.
  3. Bury the plate pieces under 1/4 inch (0.6cm) of soil.
  4. Water gently but thoroughly. Keep the soil moist, not soggy.
  5. Wait for sprouts! Most seeds germinate in 7â€“21 days.
  6. Care for your seedlings by watering regularly and ensuring sunlight.

These plates are made from natural fibers (like corn husk or pineapple crown) and embedded with seeds. They are 100% compostable and biodegradable, turning your celebration into new life for the planet.

Why Plant a Plate?

  • Sustainable: Made from agricultural by-products like corn husk or pineapple crown, reducing waste and emissions.
  • Biodegradable: Plates break down in weeks, even in landfill conditions.
  • Regenerative: Embedded seeds sprout into herbs, flowers, or native plants, supporting local ecosystems and pollinators.
  • Memorable: Each plate becomes a living reminder of your event.
